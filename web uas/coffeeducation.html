Although worldwide there are around 70 species of coffee trees, ranging in size from shrubs to trees up to 5 meters high, only 3 types of coffee beans are commonly traded and are commonly used both as single-origin coffee beans and as blends. Arabica, Robusta and Liberica coffee

 

Arabica and Robusta species are used to produce about 98% of the world's coffee production. While Liberica is a species that is quite rare and traded only 1% of the world's coffee
